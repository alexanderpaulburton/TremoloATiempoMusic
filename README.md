Piano Improvisations — MIDI Archive
Overview

I am a British-Canadian neoclassical composer and musician, maintaining an indexed archive of compositions and improvisations for research, transparency, and collaboration.

This repository contains a collection of raw MIDI piano improvisations, forming the structural basis of many of my compositions. These works are shared as compositional material, process documentation, and an open invitation for reinterpretation and study.

Timeless Improvisation

I typically begin with tempo-free improvisation as a foundation for my compositions—whether in raw classical piano, guitar, or synthetic voice-led structures. These are later developed and refined using tools such as Ableton, Audacity (still my preferred editor), and Melodyne when necessary, alongside a wider set of VST instruments and processors.

My approach to sound remains consistent: I continue to use many of the same soundfonts I first worked with over 20 years ago. This continuity forms part of my sonic identity.

Approach to Technology

While I occasionally incorporate AI into my workflow, I approach it as an extension of craft rather than a substitute. As a multi-instrumentalist with experience in composition, performance, and production, I view these tools as exploratory—augmenting, not replacing, human musicality.
